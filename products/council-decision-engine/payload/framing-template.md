# Question Framing Template

Garbage framing = garbage verdict. The advisors only know what you put in the
question — they cannot see your files, your history, or your constraints.
Spend two minutes here before you spend five seconds on the run.

## The template

```
DECISION: <one sentence — the actual choice, phrased as A vs B when possible>

CONTEXT: <who you are, what stage the project/business is at, what already exists>

CONSTRAINTS: <time per day/week, budget, skills, deadlines, anything immovable>

WHAT SPARKED THIS: <the trigger — an offer, an influencer claim, a recurring pain.
If a claim sparked it, state what you verified vs what is still hearsay>

COST OF BEING WRONG: <what happens if you pick wrong — money, months, reputation>

WHAT I THINK I WANT TO DO: <your current lean, stated honestly — the council
works best when it has a position to attack>
```

## Rules

1. **One decision per run.** "Should I do X, and also how should I price Y" is two runs.
2. **Numbers beat adjectives.** "$72/mo tier, I currently spend ~$40/mo" beats "it's kind of expensive."
3. **State your lean.** Hiding your preference doesn't make the council more objective — it makes the Contrarian aim at nothing.
4. **Name verified facts vs claims.** The council can't fact-check the internet; mark what you actually confirmed.
5. **Keep it under ~250 words.** Longer questions dilute advisor attention; move detail into the "context" line only if it changes the verdict.

## Worked example (shape, not content)

```
DECISION: Buy the $20/mo membership for tool B, the $72/mo plan for tool A, both, or neither.

CONTEXT: Solo builder, 1-2 focused hours/day, existing stack already covers
code generation and cheap-tier dispatch.

CONSTRAINTS: Monthly tool budget already at $X; no time to evaluate two new tools this month.

WHAT SPARKED THIS: An influencer story claiming a price tier that turned out
not to exist when I checked the pricing page.

COST OF BEING WRONG: ~$90/mo of dead subscription + a week of setup time.

WHAT I THINK I WANT TO DO: Buy both "to be safe" — which is exactly why I'm running this.
```
