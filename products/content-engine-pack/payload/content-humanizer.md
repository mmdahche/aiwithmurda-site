# Content Humanizer

Rebuild **voice**, not just vocabulary. Removing "delve" is step zero; the
goal is writing someone would believe a specific person typed.

## When to use

- Post-generation polish on posts, emails, landing copy
- "This sounds like AI" feedback
- Brand voice calibration on drafts

## Before starting

Gather:
- **Draft** to rewrite
- **Voice notes** — direct/casual/technical/irreverent + one example they love
- **Audience** — who reads this
- **Goal** — inform, persuade, recruit, retain

If `marketing-context.md` or brand voice doc exists, read it first.

## Voice calibration axes

| Axis | Low | High |
|------|-----|------|
| Formality | Text a friend | Board memo |
| Specificity | General principles | Named tools, numbers, dates |
| Heat | Neutral report | Clear opinion |
| Length | Punchy | Exploratory |

Pick a point on each axis — don't max everything.

## Rewrite workflow

1. **Diagnose** — list 5 tells (uniform sentence length, hedge soup, empty intensifiers)
2. **Extract claims** — every factual claim needs a source or soften to opinion
3. **Inject POV** — one sentence only the author could write from experience
4. **Cut 15%** — AI drafts are verbose; remove throat-clearing
5. **Read aloud** — stumble = rewrite that sentence

## Banned patterns (replace, don't just delete)

- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- "Leverage" / "utilize" / "delve" / "landscape" / "game-changer"
- Triple adjective stacks
- Fake intimacy ("I've been there too") without specific detail

## Ethics

- Don't invent personal stories for the author
- Don't add fake urgency ("only 3 spots left") unless true
- Preserve legal/compliance lines verbatim when flagged

## Output

Return:
1. Rewritten piece
2. **Change log** — 3 bullets on what shifted (voice, specificity, cuts)
3. **Claims audit** — list claims that still need receipts
